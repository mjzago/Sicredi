Art. 2 The electoral process will observe the provisions of the legislation, the bylaws and the rules of this Code. CHAPTER II Electoral Procedure with Delegated Vote Section I Electoral Commission Art. 3 The electoral process will be conducted by an Electoral Commission created specifically for this purpose, for each election, ensuring its autonomy and independence. § 1 The Commission will be appointed by the Board of Directors with the minimum advance notice necessary to meet all deadlines essential to the organization of the electoral process. § 2 The Commission will be made up of at least 03 (three) individual members, with the option of appointing substitutes, subject to the following requirements: a) not being part of the list of candidates; b) not be holding a position on the Board of Directors, Supervisory Board or Executive Board of the Cooperative; c) not be spouse(s), partner(s), relatives up to the 2nd (second) degree, in a direct or collateral line, of the candidates or of current mandates on the Board of Directors, Supervisory Board or Executive Board of the Cooperative; d) preferably, not be employees of the Cooperative. Art. 4 The Electoral Commission is responsible for: I - receiving the registration protocols of individual candidates and slates; II - analyze whether the registered candidates meet the legal, statutory requirements and this Code, necessary to apply for elected positions and other aspects related to the process electoral; III - approve or not the individual candidacy(s) and the registered plate(s); IV - define the procedures relating to the manifestation of candidates in the period before or during the core assemblies and general assembly; V - define whether voting will be by acclamation or secret, including the operational aspects related to the election, especially when there is more than one ticket registered, such as: a) the model of voting ballots or the electronic voting, the location of ballot boxes and voting booths, as applicable; b) the procedures for determining the results of the election, including requesting, if necessary, the support of members present to monitor the process and assist in counting the votes; SICREDI NORTE COOPERATIVA DE CREDIT Tv. VI - inform individual candidates and registered candidates of their decisions; VII - resolve omitted cases; VIII - apply the penalties provided for in this Code or in the Minutes of the Electoral Commission. § 1 The meetings will be recorded in minutes and the deliberations will be validated by the decision of a simple majority, with the majority of committee members present. § 2 At the first meeting held, the Commission will choose a coordinator and a secretary from among its members. § 3 In the absence of members of the Electoral Commission at the core assembly, the Commission will appoint a representative. § 4 The Electoral Commission may request technical assistance from areas of the Cooperative for the Commission's work. § 5º In the case of a tie in the vote for the Council of Administration or for the Supervisory Board, at the general meeting, the winner will be the one whose candidate or group of candidates has the longest average time of association with the Cooperative. § 6 Unless expressly and individually consented, in accordance with the General Personal Data Protection Law, no personal data of members will be provided for the purposes set out in this Code. Art. 5 Any registered candidate, by themselves or by persons interposed, the practice of the following acts: I - using information protected by bank secrecy or by the General Data Protection Law; II - adopt practices that may cause damage or damage to the image of Sicredi or any of the entities that make up Sicredi; III - threaten or coerce members or collaborators of the Cooperative; IV - propagate untruths or information without due proof; V - offer advantages or privileges in exchange for votes; VI - use the Sicredi brand; VII - violate current legislation, the Cooperative's Bylaws or this Code. Single paragraph. The Electoral Commission may provide, in minutes, other practices that it considers prohibited. Section II Registration to the Board of Directors and Fiscal Council Art. 7 The President of the Board of Directors will announce to the membership, at least 45 (forty-five) days before the first Core Assembly, the opening of the deadline for registration of the slate(s) for the election of the Board of Directors and individual candidacies from the Supervisory Board. § 1 The disclosure will be posted in a visible location, preferably in agencies, other forms of disclosure being provided, at the discretion of the Cooperative. This is publicity it must contain, at a minimum: a) the registration period for the individual candidacy(s) and the ticket(s), with an indication of the times; b) the place of registration; c) indication of the documents required for registration. § 2 The registration period will begin with the disclosure mentioned in this article and will end 30 (thirty) days before the first Nucleus Assembly is held, and the registration request must be filed at the Cooperative's headquarters at the time between 9 (nineteen) and 17 (seventeen) hours. § 3 The registration protocol must be accompanied by the documents required by this Code, an electronic address or contact telephone number and be signed by the individual candidate or by a member of the list, who will be the representative for all purposes. § 4 Once the registration period has closed, the Cooperative will publish the list of candidates and/or the registered slate(s) the following day. § 5 The plate(s) for the Board of Directors and candidature(s) for the Supervisory Board must be independent and complete. § 6 Individual registrations will not be accepted for candidacy for elective positions on the Board of Directors, and simultaneous participation in more than one list is prohibited. Art. 8 Once the registrations have been published, any person, whether associated or not, will have a period of 2 (two) days to present an objection, duly accompanied by the documents evidence. Single paragraph. If there is an objection, a period of 2 (two) days will be opened for the individual candidate and/or the contested ticket(s) to respond and, as soon as the deadline has expired, the documentation will be forwarded to the Electoral Commission . Art. 10. Once the registrations have been approved, the Electoral Commission must hold a meeting in order to define: I - the procedures relating to the manifestation of candidates in the period preceding or during the meetings, with the use of the Sicredi brand by any candidate; II - operational aspects related to election and voting, especially in cases where there are more than a regularly registered slate. Single paragraph. The Electoral Commission may invite individual candidates or representatives of the slates to participate in the meeting referred to in this article. Art. 11. Regardless of how members of the Board of Directors and Supervisory Boards are chosen, the registration of individual candidates or slates will not be permitted at the Core Assembly(s) or at the general assembly. CHAPTER III Final Provisions Art. 13. The deadlines provided for in this Code will be counted in calendar days, excluding the starting day and including the ending day. Single paragraph. If the due date falls on a holiday or weekend, the deadline will be considered extended. until the next business day. Art. 14. The provisions set out in this Electoral Code do not apply to the election to fill a vacant position on the Administrative or Fiscal Council already elected, in which case the replacement must be previously appointed by the respective council, observing the legal and statutory requirements .
