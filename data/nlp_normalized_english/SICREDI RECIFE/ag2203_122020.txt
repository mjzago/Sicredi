MANAGEMENT REPORT The administration of the Sicredi Recife Credit Cooperative - Sicredi Recife, in accordance with legal and statutory provisions, publishes the Financial Statements for the year ended December 31, 2020, together with the independent auditors' report on the Financial Statements. During the 2020 fiscal year, the Board of Directors and Board of Directors of Cooperative acted on numerous initiatives to contribute with the development of the community where it operates, in addition to various social actions. We can highlight the following social businesses: donations of food and personal hygiene items, in addition to the annual contribution to the Seeds of Tomorrow Project that rescues children and young people in vulnerable conditions. During this period we can highlight the following administrative facts: opening of the Olinda Shopping Patteo Agency, three more new branches under construction to open next year and hiring of 13 (thirteen) employees. Coping with the Pandemic Throughout the year we continued to face the new Coronavirus (COVID 19) pandemic in the country. Sicredi was close to the population, providing the necessary assistance to associates, employees and communities. We take care to prevent the spread of the disease, following the guidelines of the Ministry of Health and other competent bodies to combat the virus; We kept our mission alive: valuing relationships, offering financial solutions to add income and contribute to improving the quality of life of members and society. Our credit lines remained active and we also made emergency program lines available from the Government. As it is intrinsic to our business model, we closely monitor the reality of our associates and the regions where we are present, which makes it possible to expand the offering of options suited to each person's needs in facing the crisis and maintaining business. Emhuman marked by detachment human, our electronic means of service (ATMs, internet banking, app) made many of our demands viable, they are complete channels that allow the majority of operations to be carried out without the need to go to agencies. We also have telephone channels that can be used to answer questions and provide assistance via WhatsApp, which brings even more agility to many requests. Through movements such as People Who Cooperate Care and I Cooperate with the Local Economy we take care of people; We focused on the well-being of employees and associates and reaffirmed the strength of cooperation as a driver capable of contributing to the continuity of economic activity in the country. #Information classification: Internal useACTIVE 583,196 487,712 LIABILITIES 495,349 403,702 AVAILABILITY (Note 04) 485 478 DEPOSITS (Note 11) 455,337 373,824 FINANCIAL INSTRUMENTS 570,761 474,079 Demand deposits 44,917 38,822 Interbank liquidity applications (Note 05) 1,686 1,223 Interbank deposits 12 - Sicredi Recife CNPJ/MF nº 89.126.130/0001-89The execution of activities complies with the provisions in the relevant legislation, as well as to official regulatory acts, the bylaws, and internal rules of Sicredi. As of December 31, 2020, Sicredi is organized by 108 affiliated Credit Cooperatives, which operate with a service network of more than 1,990 points, of which the Cooperative operates in 4 service points. The structure also has five Regional Centers – shareholders of Sicredi Participações S.A. (“SicrediPar”) – the Confederation of Sicredi Cooperatives (“Confederação Sicredi”), a Foundation together with Banco Cooperativo Sicredi S.A (“Bank”). The Cooperative is an integral part of the Credit Cooperativism Guarantee Fund (FGCoop), a non-profit civil association, with legal personality under private law with national coverage, as per Annex I to the resolution of the National Monetary Council ("CMN") No. 4,284, of November 5, 2013. OFGCoop's purpose is to provide a guarantee for credits in cases of intervention or extrajudicial liquidation of an associated institution, up to a limit of R$250 per member (CPF/CNPJ), as well as contracting assistance, financial support and liquidity operations with these institutions. The Cooperative is also an integral part of Sicredi Fundos Garantidores, a non-profit company whose creation of reserves comes from monthly and extraordinary contributions from cooperatives associated with the fund whose purpose is to ensure the credibility and solvency of its members. According to the rules established in the Guarantee Fund Regulations, monthly contributions are determined by the sum of two installments: fixed portion, related to the objective of each Fund; and variable portion, relating to the risk attributed to the System (considers levels of liquidity, capital margin and use of security devices). NOTE 02 – PRESENTATION OF FINANCIAL STATEMENTS NOTE 01 – OPERATIONAL CONTEXT The Sicredi Recife Credit Cooperative -Sicredi Recife ("Cooperative"), is a cooperative financial institution, affiliated to the Central Cooperative deCrédito do Norte/Nordeste -Central Sicredi Norte/Nordeste (“Central”) and member of the Sicredi Cooperative System (“Sicredi”). Non-banking financial institution, authorized to operate by the Central Bank of Brazil ("Bacen") with beginning of activities on 12/29/1993 and headquarters located at Avenida Lins Petit, 100, Sls01e02, in the city of Recife - Pernambuco. The main objectives of the Cooperative are: i) Develop savings programs, adequate use of credit and provision of services, carrying out all operations active, passive and accessory credit cooperatives; ii) Provide, through mutuality, financial assistance to members in their specific activities; iii) Work on the educational training of its members, with a view to promoting cooperativism. NOTE 03 – SUMMARY OF MAIN PRACTICES The main accounting practices adopted for the preparation of the financial statements were: a) Calculation of results Income and expenses, as well as revenues and expenses, are recorded monthly in accordance with the accrual regime, which establishes that income and expenses must be included in the calculation of results for the periods in which occur, always simultaneously when they are correlated, regardless of receipt or payment, allocated in a proportional according to the amounts of gross income from cooperative action and gross income from non-cooperative action, when not identified with each activity. According to Law No. 5,764/71, the result is segregated into cooperative acts, those practiced between cooperatives and their members or by cooperatives among themselves, to achieve their statutory objectives, and non-cooperative acts, those that involve operations with non-associated third parties. Financial statements have been prepared in accordance with the accounting practices adopted in Brazil, observing the accounting guidelines issued by Law nº 6,404/76, changes introduced by Laws nº 11,638/07 and nº 11,941/09 and in line with the guidelines established by Bacen and CMN, embodied in the Accounting Plan of the Institutions of the National Financial System - COSIF and the new pronouncements, guidelines and interpretations issued by the Accounting Pronouncements Committee - CPC, approved by Bacen (CPC 01,03,04,05,10,23,24,25e27), specifically those applicable to cooperative entities and Cooperativism Law No. 5,764 of December 16, 1971 and Complementary Law 130 of April 17, 2009. Additionally, as of January 2021, the amendments to CMN Resolution No. 4,818/2020 and Bacen Resolution No. 2/20 were included in the presentation of the financial statements. The main objective of these changes is to bring similarity with the guidelines for presenting statements in accordance with international accounting standards, International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS). The main changes implemented were: the Balance Sheet accounts are presented in order of liquidity and liability and are no longer segregated into Current and Non-Current, with the segregation presented only in the Explanatory Note; The Balance Sheet balances for the period are presented in comparison with the end of the immediately previous fiscal year and the other statements are compared with the same periods of the fiscal year previous. Authorization to complete these financial statements was granted by the Board of Directors on February 10, 2021. d) Interbank relations – Financial centralization Resources raised by the Cooperative not invested in its activities are centralized through interbank transfers to the Central Cooperative, which are used by it for investments financial. These operations are characterized as cooperative acts, by Law No. 5,764/71, which defines the policy national cooperative movement. #Classification of information: Internal Useh) Investments They are stated at acquisition cost, refer to participation in companies in the Sicredi System, adjusted by provision for losses when applicable. i) Fixed assets for use Corresponds to rights that have as their object tangible assets intended for the maintenance of activities or exercised for this purpose. Depreciation of fixed assets in use is computed using the linear method, based at the annual rates mentioned in the Note "Fixed assets and intangible assets", which take into account the economic useful life of the assets.g) Other current and long-term assets (non-current) Stated at acquisition cost, including, when applicable, income earned and monetary variations daily rate incurred and exchange rate variations, deducted from the corresponding provisions for losses or adjustment to market value and income to be appropriated.e) Credit operations are demonstrated at cost plus income earned. Credit operations are classified according to the Administration's analysis regarding the level of risk, considering the economic situation and the specific risks in relation to the operations, debtors and guarantors, observing the parameters established in CMN Resolutions No. 2,682/99 and No. 2,697/00. The update (“accrual”) of credit operations due within 59 days is accounted for in income from credit operations, and from the 60th day onwards, amendments to appropriate. Operations classified as level “H” remain in this classification for six months, when they are then written off against the existing provision and controlled, for at least five years, in clearing accounts, no longer appearing on the balance sheet. f) Provision for expected losses associated with credit risk Provision for expected losses associated with credit risk is based on the analysis of operations and takes into account the economic situation, past experience, specific and global risks of the portfolios, considering the provisioning criteria, defined in Resolutions nº 2,682/99 and nº 2,697/00 of the CMN, associated with the assessments carried out by the Administration, in determining credit risks. At the municipal level, the cooperative is subject to the Tax on Services of Any Nature (ISSQN), calculated based on revenue from non-cooperative acts, that is, as a result of the provision of services to non-members; rates vary between 2% and 5% and are determined by the legislation in force in each municipality. Income resulting from operations carried out with members is tax-free. Term deposits are made up of amounts agreed for availability within pre-established deadlines, which receive updates for remunerative financial charges in accordance with their post-fixed contract and are demonstrated by their redemption value, net of ongoing financial expenses. m) Other liabilities current and long-term liabilities (non-current) Stated at known or estimated values, including, when applicable, charges and monetary variations on a pro-rata basis incurred daily, deducted from the corresponding expenses to be appropriated. n) Taxes and contributions Income Tax (IRPJ) and Social Contribution on Net Profit (CSLL) are levied on the positive result in non-cooperative acts; In this case, the provision corresponds to the current rates for IRPJ (15%, plus additional deduction of 10%) and CSLL (15%), applied to the profit earned, after the calculation basis adjustments provided for in the legislation and the offset of tax losses and negative CSLL calculation basis from previous years, this deduction being limited to 30% of the taxable profit. Still at the federal level, cooperatives are subject to contributions to the Social Integration Program (PIS –0.65%) and Contribution to Social Security Financing (COFINS –4%) on revenues earned in operations with non-members, after deduction of specific items provided for by legislation, such as construction works determined in the Statements of Surplus or Losses (DSP). j) Intangible Assets Corresponds to acquired rights that have as their object intangible assets intended for the maintenance of the System or exercised for this purpose. It is shown at cost values ​​and includes expenses on the acquisition and development of software, adjusted by accumulated amortizations, calculated from the moment in which the benefits begin to be enjoyed. respective benefits, based on annual rates that take into account the economic useful life of the assets, as mentioned in the Note "Fixed assets in use and intangible assets". k) Reduction in the recoverable value of assets Assets are reviewed annually to identify evidence of non-recoverable losses, or whenever events or changes in circumstances indicate that the carrying value may not be recoverable. When this is the case, the recoverable amount is calculated to check whether there is a loss. When there is a loss, it is recognized by the amount by which the asset's carrying value exceeds its recoverable value, which is the greater of the net sales price and the value in use of an asset. l) Interbank deposits and transfers Sight deposits are made up of values ​​whose availability is immediate to associates, therefore without a fixed period for moving them, leaving the resource holder's discretion to do so according to their needs. Capital management of the Sicredi System institutions is centralized at Banco Cooperativo Sicredi, through a structure compatible with the nature of the operations, the complexity of the products and the dimension of the System's risk exposure. The definition also includes the legal risk associated to inadequacy or deficiencies in contracts signed by the institution, to sanctions due to non-compliance with legal provisions and to compensation for third-party damages resulting from activities carried out by the institution. V - Credit Risk Management Credit risk consists of the process of identifying, measuring, controlling and mitigating risks arising from credit operations carried out by financial institutions. At Sicredi, Credit Risk management is carried out by a centralized structure and by local areas and bodies. Banco Cooperativo Sicredi is responsible for the set of policies, strategies and methodologies aimed at controlling and managing the exposure to credit risk of the companies that make up the System, Its main duties are: to respond to corporate credit risk management policies; develop and propose credit risk classification methodologies, including through quantitative models; assess and control capital requirements to cover assumed credit risk; and carry out constant monitoring of exposures subject to credit risk of all Sicredi companies.• Clear rules for classifying the trading portfolio that guarantee the correct processing of operations; • Procedures designed to measure, monitor and maintain exposure to market risk at levels considered acceptable by the Institution; • Processes designed to monitor and report adherence to the Institution's market risk appetite in relation to its capital; • Definition of market risk methodologies to be applied; • Systems to perform the calculation and measure risks, considering the complexity of the products and the size of the risk exposure market share of the System's institutions. IV - Liquidity Risk Understanding Liquidity Risk is essential for the sustainability of institutions that operate in the financial and capital markets and is associated with the institution's ability to finance commitments acquired at reasonable market prices and carry out its business plans with stable sources of financing. For this purpose, liquidity risk is defined as: •The possibility of the institution not being able to efficiently honor its expected and unexpected obligations, current and future, including those arising from the binding of guarantees, without affecting its daily operations and without incurring significant losses; and •The possibility of the institution not being able to negotiate the market price of a position, due to its large size in relation to the volume normally traded or due to some discontinuity in the market. Liquidity risk management of Sicredi System institutions is centralized at Banco Cooperativo Sicredi, through a structure compatible with the nature of the operations, the complexity of the products and the extent of the System's risk exposure.
