Sicredi, as of December 31, 2016, is organized by 118 Cooperatives deCrédito affiliates, which operate with a service network of more than 1,523 points. The structure also has five Regional Centers – shareholders of Sicredi Participações S.A. (“SicrediPar”) – the Interstate Confederation of Cooperatives Linked to Sicredi (“Confederação Sicredi”), a Foundation together with Banco Cooperativo Sicredi S.A (“Bank”). The Cooperative has been an integral part of the Credit Cooperative Guarantee Fund (FGCoop) since March 2014, a non-profit civil association, with legal personality under private law of national scope, as per annex I to CMN resolution no. 4,284, of November 5, 2013. OFGCoop's purpose is to provide guarantees for credits in cases of intervention or extrajudicial liquidation of an associated institution, up to the limit of R$ 250 thousand reais per member (CPF/CNPJ), as well as contracting assistance, financial support and liquidity operations with these institutions. The Cooperative is also an integral part of Sicredi Fundos Guarantors, a non-profit company whose reserve formation comes from monthly and extraordinary contributions from cooperatives associated with the fund, which aims to ensure the credibility and solvency of its members. According to the rules established in the Guarantee Fund Regulations, monthly contributions are determined by the sum of two installments: fixed portion, related to the objective of each Fund; and variable portion, relating to the risk attributed to the System (considers levels liquidity, capital margin and use of security devices). NOTE 01 – OPERATIONAL CONTEXT The Free Credit Cooperative Admission of Members Vale doCerrado -Sicredi Vale doCerrado ("Cooperative"), is a unique credit cooperative, affiliated to the Central Credit Cooperative of the Central North of Brazil -Central Sicredi Centro Norte and a member of the Sicredi Cooperative System ("Sicredi"). Non-banking financial institution, authorized to operate by the Central Bank of Brazil, which began its activities on 09/05/1989 and its main objectives are: i) Developing savings programs, appropriate use of credit and provision of services, carrying out all active, passive and accessory operations typical of credit cooperatives; ii) Provide, through mutuality, financial assistance to members in their specific activities; iii) Work on the educational training of its members, with a view to promoting cooperativism. Classification of Information: Unrestricted Usej) Fixed assets Corresponds to rights that have as their object tangible assets intended for the maintenance of activities or exercised for this purpose. The accounting practices for recording, measuring and disclosing contingent assets and liabilities are embodied in CMN Resolution No. 3,535/08, namely: •Contingent assets are recognized only when there are real guarantees or favorable court decisions, passed by court. Contingent assets with probable success are only disclosed in an explanatory note; •Liabilities contingents are provisioned when losses are assessed as probable and the amounts involved are measurable with sufficient certainty. Contingent liabilities assessed as possible losses are disclosed, and those classified as remote losses are not provisioned and/or disclosed; • Legal obligations are recorded as enforceable, regardless of the assessment of the probabilities of success. q) Accounting estimates Demonstrated by known or calculable values, including, when applicable, charges and monetary variations on a daily pro-rata basis incurred, deducted from the corresponding expenses to be appropriated. o) Taxes and contributions Provisions for Income Tax, Social Contribution, Social Integration Program - PISE and Contribution to Social Security Financing - COFINS were calculated at current rates, considering, for the respective calculation bases, the legislation relevant to each tax. NOTE 23 – RISK MANAGEMENT The Sicredi System considers risk management to be a priority in the conduct of its business activities, adopting practices in absolute accordance with the precepts of the Basel Accords. Therefore, it has specialized areas for managing these risks, centralized at Banco Cooperativo Sicredi S.A. Among the main risks managed by the institution, operational, market, liquidity and credit stand out, the structures of which are presented below: I - Operational Risk Information Classification: Unrestricted Use Market risk management of Sicredi System entities are centralized in the Bank, through a unified structure compatible with the nature of the operations, the complexity of the products and the extent of the exposure to the System's risk. The unified structure is responsible for the establishment of processes, policies and systems that support System entities in managing market risk. Management of the System's market risk is centralized at Banco Cooperativo Sicredi, under the responsibility of the Market Risk, Liquidity and Capital Allocation Management, subordinate to the Executive Directorate of Third Party Resources, Risks and Economy. The Sicredi System's market risk management processes include: • Clear portfolio classification rules negotiation processes that guarantee the correct treatment of operations; • Procedures designed to measure, monitor and maintain exposure to market risk at levels considered acceptable by the Institution; • Operational limits that define the market risk tolerance of the System Entities in relation to their capital; • Definition of market risk methodologies to be applied; • Systems to perform the calculation and measure risks, considering the complexity of the products and the dimension of exposure to market risk of the System Entities. III - Liquidity RiskThe establishment and dissemination of guidelines, tools and methodologies relating to operational risk for the entire System is centralized in the Superintendence of Risks and Economics of Banco Cooperativo Sicredi, subordinate to the Directorate of Third Party Resources, Risks and Economics. Regarding responsibility for managing the discipline, the structure is decentralized, that is, each entity in the System must appoint a director responsible to the Central Bank. II - Market Risk Market risk is defined as the possibility of losses resulting from fluctuations in the market values ​​of deposits held by a financial institution. This definition includes operations subject to the risks of exchange rate variation, interest rates, share prices and commodity prices. For the purposes of current legislation, Capital Management is defined as the continuous process of: • Monitoring and control of the capital maintained by the Institution; • Assessment of the need for capital to face the risks to which the Institution is subject; • Planning goals and capital needs, considering the Institution's strategic objectives. The capital management of the Sicredi System entities are centralized in the Bank, through a unified structure compatible with the nature of the operations, the complexity of the products and the dimension of risk exposure of the system. The unified structure is responsible for establishing the processes, policies and systems that support System entities in capital management. Processes and policies for capital management are established following the minimum criteria established by current regulations, aligned with best market practices, and approved by the competent authorities of each entity in the System. Understanding Liquidity Risk is essential for the sustainability of institutions that operate in the financial and capital markets and is associated with the institution's ability to finance commitments acquired at reasonable market prices and carry out its business plans with stable sources of financing. For this purpose, liquidity risk is defined as: •The possibility of the institution not being able to efficiently honor its expected and unexpected obligations, current and future, including those arising from the binding of guarantees, without affecting its daily operations and without incurring in significant losses and; •The institution may not be able to negotiate the market price of a position, due to its large size in relation to the volume normally traded or due to some discontinuity in the market. The liquidity risk management of Sicredi System entities is centralized at the Bank, through a unified structure compatible with the nature of the operations, the complexity of the products and the dimension of the System's risk exposure. The unified structure is responsible for establishing the processes, policies and systems that support System entities in managing liquidity risk. The processes and policies for managing liquidity risk are established following the minimum criteria established by current regulations, aligned with best market practices, and approved by the competent authorities of each entity in the System. The processes for managing the Sicredi System's liquidity risk include: • Definition of processes to identify, assess, monitor and control exposure to liquidity risk over different time horizons; • Establishment of operational limits to maintain adequate and sufficient levels of liquidity; • Definition of funding strategies that provide adequate diversification of funding sources and maturity dates; •Definition of a liquidity contingency plan, regularly updated, that establishes responsibilities and procedures to deal with liquidity stress situations; • Periodic carrying out stress tests with short and long-term scenarios.
